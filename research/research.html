I am a Ph.D. student of Physics in University of Warsaw. My PhD thesis is about pattern formation in dissolving rocks. In carbonate rocks, a reactant such as acid rain, in certain physical conditions, can form highly conductive flow channels known as wormholes—not the ones related to Einstein's general relativity theory. In nature, these patterns are typically known as karst features. Some commonly known examples are caves, solution pipes and sink-holes
